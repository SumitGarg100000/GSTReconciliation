How to Use the Tool

  1. Download the sample Excel file from the link to understand the format.
  2. Copy and paste your GSTR-2B data into the "GST Portal" sheet from column B.
  3. Copy and paste your GSTR-3B or books data into the "Client Data" sheet from column B.
  4. Upload your Excel file on the GST Reconciliation Tool website.
  5. Set the "Difference Allowed" value (default is 1) to adjust matching tolerance.
  6. Review the tables (GSTR-2B, GSTR-3B, and Summary) for results.
  7. User can edit values directly on website if needed.
  8. Download the reconciled output file by clicking "Download Reconciled Data".
  9. For support, contact Sumit Garg via WhatsApp 9716804520 (click on WhatsApp icon).

Matching Heading Criteria


  1. GSTN, Invoice No. & Date - Means data will be matched based on the total of GSTN, Invoice No., and Date.
  2. Invoice Number - Means match data will be matched based on the total of GSTN and Invoice No.
  3. GSTN & Date - Means match data will be matched based on the total of GSTN and Date.
  4. GSTN - Means match data will be matched based on the total of GSTN.
  5. GSTN Not Match - Means the GST Portal sheet has some GSTN that is not available in the Client Data sheet, or vice versa.
  6. Not Match Any Criteria - Means the GST Portal sheet has some GSTN (available in both sheets) that appears in the Client Data sheet but doesnâ€™t match any criteria, or vice versa (possible difference in amounts).
